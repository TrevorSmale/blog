About

Go is a statically typed compiled language touted as 'C for the 21st century' due in part to its development taking place after the advent of the internet, distributed databases and compute, as well as large organizational engineering projects.

Initial development took place between Rob pike (C/UTF 8) Robert Griesemer (Hotspot JVM) Ken Thompson (Unix/Bsd) at Google starting in 2007. They were trying to solve a fundamental problem google was facing, many languages and frameworks were needed to maintain the large distributed computing infrastructure google had and a singular language was needed that could handle a broad range of new tasks. Human readable syntax, Memory locality and Concurrency were top of the priority list.

Features of Golang

Concurrency - Allowing for many tasks to virtually run in parellel

Distribituted Systems - Many devices accross many networks

Garbage Collection - Erasing memory loops

Memory Locality - Specifying memory pools

Readability - Much easier to comprehend the intent of the program
