About

Go is a statically typed compiled language touted as 'C for the 21st century' due in part to its development taking place after the advent of the internet and distributed computing.

Initial Development

Took place between Rob pike (C/UTF 8) Robert Griesemer (Hotspot JVM) Ken Thompson (Unix/Bsd) at Google starting in 2007. Many languages and frameworks were needed to maintain the large distributed computing infrastructure google had. There was a desire for a modern language that could handle just about anything.

Go is specifically built for

Concurrency - Allowing for many tasks to virtually run in parellel

Distribituted Systems - Many devices accross many networks

Garbage Collection - Erasing memory loops

Memory Locality - Specifying memory pools

Readability - Much easier to comprehend the intent of the program
